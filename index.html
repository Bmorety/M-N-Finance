Internal — Client Persona

Who We're Talking To

Before writing anything, sending anything, or saying anything — know this person. Everything we produce is calibrated to them.

Profile
The Owner. 45–65 years old. Runs a business with 15–200 employees. Has been doing this for 10–30 years. Built it themselves or inherited it from family. High on instinct, often low on formal finance — reads P&L but doesn't always trust it.
Industry
Manufacturing, automotive supply chain (Tier 1–3), traditional services, logistics, industrial SMBs. Often family-owned. Often in regions with concentrated industrial exposure — automotive corridor, food processing, construction supply.
Current State of Mind
Under pressure — but not panicking. Margins are tighter. Costs are up. AI news makes them uneasy. EV disruption, Mercosur, rising energy costs — they see the headlines. They're watching. They want to know: how strong am I actually?
What They Actually Want
Clarity. To know if the business is solid. To not be surprised. To understand if they can exit in 3–5 years — or if they need to fix something first. And honestly? To talk to someone who actually understands their kind of business.
What They Don't Want
Another consultant who doesn't know their industry. Jargon. A slide deck that says "let's align on strategic pillars." Someone who talks about vision while they're dealing with a cash flow gap. They can smell bullshit immediately.
Trigger Moments
Bank requests updated financials. Key customer is at risk. An offer to buy the business lands unexpectedly. A family member needs to be brought in or bought out. They wake up and realize they don't actually know what the business is worth.
Internal — Tone & Voice

How We Sound

Every word we write or say should pass the test: "Would a stressed owner roll their eyes at this?" If yes — cut it.

Direct, not blunt for shock value
Say: "Your DSO is 68 days. That's eating your cash." Not: "We leverage dynamic receivables optimisation frameworks."
Colleague, not consultant
We're the person they call before they call their lawyer. We pick up the phone. Not the one who sends a 40-page report and disappears.
Honest about limits
If something isn't our area, we say so. That's what earns trust. Don't oversell. Don't pitch services they don't need yet.
Serious, not solemn
Finance is stressful. Dry humour is fine. Existential gravitas is not. We're not saving their soul. We're fixing their numbers.
Words we never use
synergies pivot leverage (verb) ecosystem journey best-in-class value proposition holistic mission-driven unlock elevate stakeholders
Words we like
numbers control clear direct margin exit cash owner honest real fix picture

Internal — Sales Process

How a Deal Gets Done

No tricks. No pressure. No pipeline theatre. This is how we move from an interested owner to a signed client.

Step 01
First contact or referral
Owner reaches out or is referred by a banker, lawyer, or accountant. First response within 24h — voice note or short personal email, not a template. Goal: establish "we're not like the others" in the first 2 minutes.
Step 02
Financial Health Score (the hook)
Send the link or do it live together on a call. Lowest-friction entry — no commitment, clear value. Always offer: "Even if we don't work together, this is worth doing." Use the score to frame the 45-min conversation.
Step 03
45-minute review call
Go through the numbers together — don't just present them back, ask questions. Find the one thing that's actually costing them the most. End with a specific, concrete recommendation — not a vague next step. If it's a fit, say so plainly.
Step 04
One-page proposal
If there's a fit: send a one-pager within 48 hours. What we'll do, what they'll get, what it costs. No Powerpoint. No 20-page methodology doc. One page, plain language, 48 hours — never a week later.
Step 05
Close or close the loop
If yes: simple agreement, kick-off call within 1 week. If no: "When would be a better time to revisit?" — set a date. Don't chase. Always ask for a referral after 30 days of a successful engagement. Referrals are our best channel.
